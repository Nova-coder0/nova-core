# NOVA No-BNB Growth Plan

The current constraint is simple: serious PancakeSwap liquidity requires a second asset such as BNB or USDT. Code cannot create real BNB. The correct strategy is to build value and trust first, then attract or earn liquidity.

## What Is Not Acceptable

- No exploits.
- No fake liquidity.
- No fake volume.
- No wash trading.
- No price manipulation.
- No promises that the token price will rise.

## Legal Paths

### 1. Utility First

Build something useful before asking anyone to provide liquidity.

Immediate utilities:

- NOVA transparency dashboard.
- Holder monitor.
- Treasury and owner-wallet alerting.
- Telegram/Discord bot.
- API-credit prototype powered by NOVA.

If the project is useful, liquidity becomes easier to justify.

### 2. BNB Chain Grants

BNB Chain has official builder and grant programs. The grant path should focus on open-source public-good tooling, not price.

Grant angle:

- open-source token transparency dashboard,
- BNB Chain token risk monitor,
- liquidity and owner-wallet alert tooling,
- educational tooling for small token creators.

Sources:

- BNB Chain Grants: https://www.bnbchain.org/en/grants
- BNB Chain Builder Programs: https://www.bnbchain.org/en/programs

### 3. Community Liquidity

After tokenomics and risks are public, supporters can voluntarily add liquidity.

Rules:

- no guaranteed returns,
- no pressure,
- no hidden deals,
- public pool address,
- public LP policy,
- public risk explanation.

### 4. Partner Liquidity

A partner can contribute BNB if NOVA contributes tokens.

Required terms:

- written public agreement,
- vesting or lock,
- transparent wallet addresses,
- no secret discount that harms the community.

### 5. Earn BNB Through Product Work

The project can earn BNB or stablecoins by offering useful services:

- custom token dashboards,
- monitoring bots,
- contract transparency reports,
- small BNB Chain analytics tools.

Revenue can then fund liquidity honestly.
